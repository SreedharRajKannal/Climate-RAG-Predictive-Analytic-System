CC-DOC-006: Emergency Readiness and Multi-Hazard Governance
Target Metadata: category: disaster_governance, variable: emergency_preparedness

Institutional Warning Systems:
* India Meteorological Department (IMD) Color Coding Framework:
  * Green (No Warning): Baseline operations. No systemic action required.
  * Yellow (Be Updated): Equivalent to Level 2 (Caution). Approaching critical thresholds; verify response readiness.
  * Orange (Be Prepared): Equivalent to Level 3 (Warning). High probability of severe weather conditions; secure assets and restrict transit.
  * Red (Take Action): Equivalent to Level 4 (Critical). Imminent or active severe weather events; implement emergency survival protocols immediately.

Strategic Kit Configuration:
* Caloric and Hydration Reserves: Maintain an emergency supply kit containing non-perishable food items with high caloric densities. Store potable water allowing a minimum of 4 Liters per person per day, dedicated strictly to metabolic hydration and hygiene requirements.
* Telemetry and Communication: Equip kits with high-intensity LED torches, spare batteries, a comprehensive first-aid kit, and a dedicated mobile communications device. Pre-program the device with emergency contact numbers, including national disaster hotlines and local emergency services.

Vulnerability Distribution Networks:
* Community Resilience Integration: When an Orange or Red alert is issued, broadcast weather notifications across family, neighborhood, and community networks. Prioritize reaching vulnerable groups, such as mobility-impaired individuals, elderly residents, and families living in temporary structures.
